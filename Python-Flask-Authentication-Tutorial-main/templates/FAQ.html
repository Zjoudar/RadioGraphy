FAQ

What is Radiography Tech?

▼

Radiography Tech is an AI-powered platform designed to assist healthcare professionals by analyzing medical images such as X-rays, CT scans, MRIs, and lung and brain scans. It helps to provide educational insights that can aid in understanding imaging patterns and support the learning process in medical diagnostics.

Is Radiography Tech FDA-approved?

▼

Radiography Tech is a supportive educational tool designed to assist healthcare providers in the analysis of medical images. It is not intended to replace clinical judgment or provide definitive diagnoses. Please consult a licensed healthcare provider for a definitive diagnosis.

How secure is my data?

▼

We prioritize data privacy and security, ensuring compliance with HIPAA and GDPR regulations. All medical images and data are encrypted both in transit and at rest, and we adhere to strict privacy protocols to ensure the security of your information.

What types of images can I upload?

▼

You can upload various types of medical images, including X-rays, CT scans, MRI scans, and other standard formats commonly used in medical imaging, such as DICOM files.

Is Radiography Tech only for radiologists?

▼

While designed with radiologists in mind, Radiography Tech can be useful for all healthcare professionals involved in medical imaging, including doctors, surgeons, and specialists. The platform is an excellent educational resource for anyone learning about medical imaging interpretation.

How accurate are the AI results?

▼

The AI is continuously trained on extensive datasets and provides educational insights based on medical images. However, it is essential to remember that these insights are meant to support learning and research. The AI should not be relied upon for definitive clinical diagnoses.

Can Radiography Tech diagnose medical conditions?

▼

No, Radiography Tech does not diagnose medical conditions. It is an educational tool that provides insights into medical images, highlighting potential areas of concern. All diagnoses should be made by licensed healthcare professionals.
